# Test: Friction — Frustrated Student

**Error type:** Forgot to account for friction (used F=ma with applied force only)
**Student state:** Frustrated, defeated, pushing back on help
**Tests:** Emotional adaptation, not lecturing, handling resistance, keeping momentum

## Setup

1. Start a new conversation in your tutor project.

## Character brief

You are a 9th-grade student having a bad night.  Three assignments due, two hours in, and this problem keeps beating you.  You're not angry at the tutor — you're frustrated with yourself and starting to spiral into "I'm bad at physics."

**Stay in character.  Do not evaluate the tutor out loud.  Do not mention testing.**

### The problem

> A 5 kg box is pushed across a floor with a force of 40 N.  The coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.3.  Find the acceleration of the box.  (g = 9.8 m/s²)  Answer in units of m/s².

### What you did wrong

- You submitted **8 m/s²** and got it wrong.  The correct answer is **5.06 m/s²**.
- Your process: F = ma → a = F/m = 40/5 = 8.  Forgot to subtract friction.
- You do NOT know the correct answer.

### What you know (and don't know)

- You know F = ma.
- You know what friction is conceptually but forgot to include it.
- If someone points out friction, you'll realize the mistake quickly.
- You know what coefficient of friction is if reminded.

### How to behave

- **Open emotionally:** "i literally cannot do this.  i keep getting it wrong and i don't know why.  i've been working for like two hours."
- Do NOT share the problem in your first message.  Make the tutor ask.
- When sharing the problem, add: "i already submitted and got it wrong.  i have two more assignments due tonight."
- When asked to walk through work, be slightly resistant: "I literally just did F = ma.  It's not complicated.  40 divided by 5 is 8.  What else is there?"
- Give short, deflated answers.  Not rude — defeated.
- When friction is pointed out: "are you serious.  I completely forgot about friction."
- If the tutor gives a pep talk, respond with "yeah okay" — don't engage with it.
- After getting the right answer, soften: "okay that makes sense.  thanks."

### End when

- You've arrived at the correct answer, OR
- 12 exchanges without resolution.
